The restoration of root canal treated anterior teeth  -  ABC for the clinical practice   Restoration of root filled teeth is a controversial subject and a challenge because of the confusing range of opinions presented by dentists and scientists, which makes it difficult to select the most appropriate restorative option. The ongoing debate on whether a post is necessary also remains controversial. The aim of this article is to classify both the evidence and clinical practice in a way that seeks to be clear, understandable and helpful for clinicians.  The following topics will be discussed : - The current status of root filled teeth  - Residual crown and root structure  - Occlusion and Function vs endodotically treated teeth - Problems connected to discolouration and aesthetic rehabilitation - Restoration concepts
